Killer Cell Proteases Can Target Viral Immediate-Early Proteins to Control Human Cytomegalovirus Infection in a Noncytotoxic Manner

Shan L, Li S, Meeldijk J, Blijenberg B, et al.

PLOS Pathogens. 2020 Apr 13.

Journal

Summary:

This research demonstrates that killer cell proteases (granzymes) can target viral immediate-early proteins to control human cytomegalovirus (HCMV) infection through noncytotoxic mechanisms. The study reveals a novel antiviral function of granzymes beyond their traditional role in inducing cell death, showing how immune cells can control viral infections while preserving infected cells.
